# Case Study 01: 4× Content Output for B2B SaaS Company

**Industry:** B2B SaaS — HR Technology  
**Company Size:** 85 employees, Series B  
**Engagement Type:** AI Content System Implementation  
**Duration:** 8 weeks

---

## Situation

A Series B HR-tech company needed to scale content marketing to support a sales-led growth motion. With a content team of two and a pipeline target that had grown 3× year-over-year, they were producing 4 pieces of content per month — far below what was needed to fuel organic growth and support sales enablement.

The team was spending 60–70% of their time on research and first drafts, leaving almost no time for strategy or distribution.

---

## Before State

| Metric | Baseline (Q3 2023) |
|--------|-------------------|
| Content pieces per month | 4 |
| Average hours per piece | 18 hours |
| Monthly organic sessions | 12,400 |
| MQLs from organic content | 28/month |
| Cost per content MQL | $847 |
| SEO keyword rankings (top 10) | 61 |

**Team structure:** 1 content manager, 1 SEO specialist, 1 agency for design

**Primary bottleneck:** First draft production. 8–10 hours per piece before any editing.

---

## Approach

### Phase 1 (Weeks 1–2): Audit and System Design
- Audited existing content library (94 pieces) to identify what drove organic MQLs
- Mapped buyer journey to identify content gaps by stage and persona
- Documented brand voice in AI-ready format (examples, anti-examples, tone guidelines)

### Phase 2 (Weeks 3–4): AI Production Workflow
Implemented a 5-step AI-augmented workflow:
1. **Topic identification:** Claude analysis of top SERP content + keyword gaps
2. **AI briefing:** Automated brief generation from keyword + intent + competitive analysis
3. **Research synthesis:** AI aggregation of 5–10 source materials per piece
4. **First draft:** Claude-generated draft from brief + research (45 min vs. 8 hours)
5. **Human edit:** 2-hour human edit for original perspective, accuracy, and voice

### Phase 3 (Weeks 5–6): SEO Optimization Layer
- Integrated Surfer SEO into the workflow for on-page optimization
- Built prompt templates for meta descriptions, title tags, and heading structure
- Created internal linking system using AI to identify link opportunities across library

### Phase 4 (Weeks 7–8): Scale and Measurement
- Trained content team on the new workflow
- Built production dashboard tracking pieces/week and time-per-piece
- Established quality rubric with 10-point editorial checklist

---

## After State (3 months post-implementation)

| Metric | Before | After | Change |
|--------|--------|-------|--------|
| Content pieces per month | 4 | 17 | **+325%** |
| Average hours per piece | 18 hrs | 4.5 hrs | **−75%** |
| Monthly organic sessions | 12,400 | 18,900 | **+52%** |
| MQLs from organic content | 28/mo | 71/mo | **+154%** |
| Cost per content MQL | $847 | $312 | **−63%** |
| SEO keyword rankings (top 10) | 61 | 148 | **+143%** |

**Annualized content MQL impact:** ~516 incremental MQLs at $312 CPL vs. $847 = ~$277K in efficiency savings

---

## Implementation Notes

**What worked exceptionally well:**
- Voice documentation was the highest-leverage investment — better input = dramatically better output
- The 2-hour human edit was non-negotiable for quality and should not be compressed further
- Separating "research" and "draft" into distinct AI steps produced significantly better results than one-shot drafting

**Watch out for:**
- AI hallucinates statistics — every data point needs verification
- First 2–3 pieces require more editing time; calibration improves quickly
- SEO metrics lag 60–90 days — set stakeholder expectations appropriately

**Tools used:**
- Claude (Anthropic) — Research synthesis + drafting
- Surfer SEO — On-page optimization
- Notion — Brief templates and content calendar
- Ahrefs — Keyword research and competitor analysis
